Executive Summary

The Professional Development Committee (PD) of the Master of Information Student Council (MISC) requested the MISC Survey Committee to ask students about what PD can do to help improve their events that cater to the professional development needs of students at the Faculty of Information. The survey questions and form were designed in close collaboration with PD members.

Methods

The Professional Development Survey was launched on November 4 and was initially scheduled to end on November 19, during the Fall term. However, it was extended until December 1 to gather more responses from students. The survey collected valid responses from 26 Master’s degree students at the Faculty of Information. Students were informed that by submitting the survey, they consent to their responses being summarized, analyzed, and quoted anonymously for a MISC report. The survey contained 1 iSchool screener question, for which 26 verified participants responded correctly (2 participants that answered incorrectly were removed), and 5 multiple-choice demographics questions regarding participants’ program and enrolment status. All participants were asked 2 questions (long-answer) about challenges they face with professional development, and 8 questions (multiple-choice and long-answer) about their opinions or suggestions in regards to professional development events. Because many questions were optional, the number of responses for each question varied.

The survey was promoted through MISC-y Business newsletter and the iSchool Discord. Participants were incentivized with a chance to win a $10 digital gift card from a retailer of their choice if they entered a raffle at the end of the survey. There were 29 raffle entrants, including 5 entrants that were removed due to their failure to provide a UofT email, leaving only 24 verified entrants. A total of 2 verified raffle winners were picked.

Main Findings

Demographics

  • 96.1% (25) of 26 total participants were Master of Information (MI) students, while 1 was a Combined Degree Program (CDP) student.
  • The LIS concentration made up the biggest portion of the respondents, with 38.5% (10) of total participants, followed by UXD with 34.6% (9 participants).
  • 52% (13) of 25 respondents began their iSchool program in 2020, while 32% (8) began in 2021.
  • 44% (11) of 25 respondents were not doing co-op or thesis as part of their MI program, 32% (8) have chosen to do co-op, and 20% (5) have not decided.
  • 80.8% (21) of all 26 participants were enrolled full-time.

Student Needs

  • Current professional development challenges that participants highlighted include networking, job searching and obtaining relevant positions, and a general sense of uncertainty about where to start with job search and professional development.
  • For professional development support, students reported seeking events or workshops that offered practical skills or preparation for improving their job applications (e.g. interview prep for their specific disciplines), networking opportunities, learning hard/technical skills required in their desired job positions/fields (especially for LIS concentration students), and career pathway workshops (e.g. ‘what I can do with my HCDS degree’).
  • Students also listed some needs that were not in PD Committee’s event ideas in the next section of the survey, such as finding mentorship, having more opportunities to interact with faculty members, and doing collaborative projects with other students outside courses.

Professional Development Events

  • In November 2021, 61.5% (16) of respondents claimed that they feel comfortable attending in-person events in the Winter term, but this might have changed due to the recent developments in COVID-19.
  • Of the event ideas that the PD committee proposed, respondents showed the highest level of interest in Alumni/Industry Round Tables (65.4% or 17 respondents answered “very interested”).
  • Other events that many participants were interested in include Personal Branding Review (e.g. LinkedIn profile, website, etc), Tips on Prep Applications and Interviews, Networking with Other Students, and Virtual Study Hall (Independent Projects).
  • 53.8% (14) participants were not interested in Resources Overview (CLNx, Indeed, etc.) and Virtual Study Hall (Portfolio/Resumes).

Recommendations

  • When prioritizing professional development events to organize for students, the PD Committee might want to consider that students in different concentrations could have very different needs, and each concentration might not be proportionally represented by the survey participants. For example, UXD students might be very interested in Portfolio Reviews, but the event is not relevant for students in other concentrations, and UXD students make up only 9 of 26 participants in this survey.
  • In the long-answer questions, students mentioned many concentration-specific needs that have not been met through courses, thus the PD Committee might want to consider organizing concentration-specific events.

2. Student Needs

This section asked students about their current challenges and needs in professional development before the survey presents them with ideas of resources and events that could help them.

Participants were asked two long-answer questions before the survey presented them with a list of event ideas that the PD Committee came up with. This is to see what the participants’ claimed to be their current needs without the bias of the survey’s proposing what their needs might be.

What challenges are you facing with professional development?

17 responses

Networking was the most common challenge reported by 6 of the 17 respondents, with one student also highlighting mentorship. Some of the difficulties participants highlighted include finding networking intimidating, having limited opportunities to interact with students and faculty, especially in-person or in a hybrid setting, and continuing a long-term relationship with a network connection.

“Not being good at making connections. I just feel like I don't know the right people to get the opportunities I'm looking for? … having a mentor that could help guide me along the learning process would be amazing.”

“Continuing networking relationships after first contact, development of soft skills (ex. Conflict resolution)

4 respondents also noted challenges faced related to job searching and gaining relevant work experience to build towards a permanent job or career after graduating. One of the respondents highlighted how this is more difficult for students who also need to work to support themselves while studying in the program.

“Even though I'm doing a relevant work-study job, I'm afraid I don't have enough work experience to get a full-time UX job. I changed my concentration after the first year, so I don't qualify for a UXD co-op.”

“Funding my education while also getting relevant experience. I can fund my education, but the work I do is not relevant to my future plans. I can pursue my future plans, but then I cannot pay for my education.”

“I feel like remote positions have in many ways made things easier … [but] now more people are able to apply to positions than before[…] You find a remote position that you're interested in, and then you notice that it has over 300 applicants and from then on it feels like even getting to the interview stage is an impossibility …”

3 respondents reported challenges about not knowing where to start with their professional development due to a general lack of guidance, uncertainty, and feeling overwhelmed by the many different possibilities and things to do.

“No specific professional career support from school, I need to learn by myself almost from scratch.”

Other students mentioned that their challenges include finding it difficult to start making their portfolio (2 respondents), not feeling like they have enough experience or skills (2 students), finding PD events that fit in with a full-time work schedule, incorporating co-op with the CDP program, finding opportunities for informal interactions with faculty, and handling resume gaps caused by disability.

“Building my portfolio - the technical aspect of it (e.g., how to use webflow)”

For your specific concentration(s), what kind of professional development support would you like to have?

26 responses

Job application and professional development for specific fields

11 of 26 respondents to this optional question mentioned that they would like more help in tailoring their job search and application preparation to positions in their specific fields. For example, an interview for a UX design position may consist of some activities that are different from those in an interview for a data scientist or librarian position.

“Mock interviews, whiteboard challenges (maybe could do small groups or something), portfolio reviews, resume reviews, pointers to networking/related communities (like Design Buddies, LinkedIn influencers, conferences, etc).”

“Interview skills for data analyst, industry liaison and internal reference resource.”

“More advisories on job application for Business Analyst/Data Analyst, from resume, cover letter to interview preparation.”

Furthermore, 5 students would like to learn about specific positions that they can qualify for through their concentration in the MI program.

“Workshops discussing the range of potential jobs for HCDS graduates and how they differ. Sometimes professors talk like we will all be working under the role ‘data scientist’ in a large company, but there's a lot more out there. A wide coverage with good pros and cons, so we can tailor our professional development specifically to the direction we want to go, would be helpful.”

“Anything for KMIM - I feel that it's often ignored and I still don't have a good understanding of its complexities.”

“I would love to be exposed to more variety in terms of the kinds of positions people have been able to get with their C&T concentration. I feel like there are usually a couple of positions that I see discussed a lot, but I feel like with a wide-ranging concentration as C&T, I'd love to see more diversity in terms of the positions people have been able to get.”

4 respondents are currently primarily concerned with finding work experience, co-op or otherwise, that are relevant to their concentration.

“Advice on how to get relevant experience without co-op (UXD particularly).”

“Information about where to find library jobs. Is there a central place to find them or do you have to look at individual library websites?”

Other than just the job application process, some participants are interested in learning new skills and knowledge related to their fields that can be applied in the workplace. 5 respondents wrote that they want opportunities to learn more hard skills, and 4 of these respondents are in the LIS concentration.

“Learn how to navigate new RDA standards.”

“Practical courses to practice cataloguing, reference work, and customer service skills”

A few students also mentioned that they are interested in workshops to learn about niche topics within their fields and reaching out to professionals that work there. These topics include healthcare information, sustainability, and smart city data analytics.

In addition, there is some interest among a few respondents about how to further navigate the professional world beyond just career exploration and job applications. One student wrote that they want to learn about salaries for the position they are interested in, and another would like guidance on professional development for students who are already working and want to leverage their iSchool degrees to make a career switch.

Networking opportunities

Many students also mentioned their interest in having more networking opportunities, whether with other students (in general or within their respective concentrations), alumni, current professionals, faculty members, and potential mentors in their specific fields. In addition, a few students feel that they need to develop their networking skills, such as through workshops.

“Maybe small group discussions with a faculty member? Hard for faculty to get to know you, so it is hard for them to advocate for you beyond the iSchool.”

“Panels with library professionals would also be interesting to see.”

A few respondents also suggested events for students from different fields/skill backgrounds to collaborate on projects that they can put into their portfolios, such as hackathons.

“I'm thinking about trying to organize my software engineer friends into making a video game or some other digital product we can all use for our portfolios.”

“I also would love for MISC to help organize project groups that comprise of students from multiple concentrations that participate in a design challenge or something-- like a hackathon but iSchool specific.”

3. Professional Development Events

This section gathered feedback that the Professional Development Committee intends to use for organizing events that students still need in addition to professional development resources already provided by the university and iSchool.

When this survey was open in November 2021, 61.5% (16) participants claimed to be comfortable with attending in-person events in the coming Winter term, while 23.1% (6) were still unsure. However, due to the recent development in the pandemic, the PD Committee most likely would have to hold events virtually following the Faculty of Information and University’s policies and safety precautions until further notifications.

Figure 5. Whether participants are comfortable with attending in-person events in Winter term

Next, the survey asked participants to rate their interest level on 13 professional development event & workshop ideas, categorized as Individual Development, Networking, Job Search, and Peer Events. For each event idea, participants could choose “Very interested”, “Somewhat interested”, or “Not interested, or N/A”. (For example, participants not in the UXD concentration may not need any Portfolio Review events, thus might choose N/A).

Overall, the event that most participants show the highest level of interest in is Alumni/Industry Round Tables (65.4% or 17 respondents were very interested). Other events that many participants were interested in include Personal Branding Review (e.g. LinkedIn profile, website, etc), Tips on Prep Applications and Interviews, and, to a lower degree of interest, Networking with Other Students, and Virtual Study Hall (Independent Projects). Participants seem to be more lukewarm about Resume/Cover Letter Clinics and Headshots; most students were somewhat interested, but, also taking into consideration their qualitative responses in other questions, these may not be events that students felt the most need for. Resources Overview (CLNx, Indeed, etc.) is the event that respondents showed the least amount of interest for.

There are some events that students seem to be more polarized about, such as Employer Information Sessions, which 57.7% (15) students were very interested in but 26.9% (7) were not interested in or found to be not applicable. This could be due to respondents’ differences in field/concentrations or personal perspective on how useful Employer Information Sessions are for their respective job search processes. Similarly, students seem to be divided over their interest levels in Portfolio Reviews and Peer Review Sessions (Portfolio/Project/Resume) depending on their concentrations, as these events may be either very useful or not applicable for students depending on the field or job position they want to get into.

Do you have any event suggestions or reasoning for your choices above?

6 responses

Respondents mostly re-emphasized their needs for events and resources already mentioned in the previous sections. 2 respondents mentioned wanting more opportunities to network with alumni, 1 respondent wrote that they really do not like virtual meetings, 1 wrote that they really need help with personal branding, and 1 wanted job application preparation support specific to their field instead of generic job search advice and workshops.

“How you should write your resume is different in data science and library science; interviews for UX and policy jobs are not the same; the application process differs in large companies, small companies, nonprofits, and government. [...] I think the best thing the committee could do is offer something that you can't find on CLNx, LinkedIn, etc.-- something specific to the needs of our faculty and the diverse subgroups within it.”

Do you have any other thoughts that you want to share with the Professional Development committee?

6 responses

1 respondent reported a desire for events about the emotional aspects of the job search, as they find it quite draining:

“I feel like it would help to have events not just about the logistics or practical aspects of applying to and finding jobs--resumes, cover letters, interviews, networking, etc.--but also the emotional and psychological aspects. Looking for and applying to jobs is exhausting … so it would be nice to just have events focused on the more emotional or psychological side of things and how to support that.”

4 respondents left positive comments about the PD Committee  :-)

“I think it's wonderful that the PDC exists! PD is definitely a big concern in a professional degree, and I love to see it coming from a student angle.”

“Thanks guys :)”

“Thank you for caring”

“Appreciate your interest and creative approach”

Introduction + Screener

This survey is intended for all current Master of Information students at the University of Toronto, Faculty of Information. The aim of this survey is to direct and inform future activities of the MISC Professional Development Committee. This survey should take about 5-8 minutes to complete.

You may enter a draw for a $10 digital gift card at the end of the survey. Your participation in this survey is much appreciated!

This survey asks about student opinions on events and professional development activities. Survey responses are anonymous and do not ask for any identifying information. Some questions are optional and your eligibility for the draw will not be affected by your answers. By submitting this survey, you consent to your responses being summarized, analyzed, and quoted anonymously for a MISC report.

  4. What is/are your concentration(s)?
  5. Which MI program options are you participating in?
